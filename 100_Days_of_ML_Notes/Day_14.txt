Key Stages of Problem Framing:

Churn Prediction -> percentage of customers who stop using a product/service over a period of time.

Define the Business Goal: Identify the main problem (e.g., increasing Netflix revenue) and set a specific target (e.g., reducing churn rate from 4% to 3.75%) 

Identify the ML Task: Determine the type of problem—in this case, shifting from a classification problem (will they leave?) to a regression problem (what is the probability they will leave?) to enable personalized interventions like targeted discounts 

Evaluate Existing Solutions: Check what solutions are already in place to build upon them

Acquire Data: Identify and collect the necessary data points, such as user watch time, search history, and failed searches, often requiring collaboration with data engineers

Define Metrics: Establish key performance indicators to measure the model's success in achieving the business goal 

Choose Learning Approach: Decide between batch learning (offline training) and online learning (continuous training) based on how volatile the data is

Check Assumptions: Validate that the data required for features is actually available and that the model applies correctly to different contexts (e.g., different geographies)
